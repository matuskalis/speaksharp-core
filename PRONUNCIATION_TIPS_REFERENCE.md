# Pronunciation Tips Quick Reference

## Common Mispronunciations Database

### TH Sounds

#### 1. "the" /ðə/
**Common errors**: de, da, ze
**Tip**: Place your tongue between your teeth and blow air gently. It should feel like a soft vibration.
**Example**: Say 'the' like 'thuh', not 'duh'

#### 2. "think" /θɪŋk/
**Common errors**: tink, sink, fink
**Tip**: Place your tongue between your teeth for the 'th' sound. Practice saying 'three, think, thought'.
**Example**: Think starts with a soft 'th' sound, not 't'

#### 3. "this" /ðɪs/
**Common errors**: dis, zis
**Tip**: Tongue between teeth, make it vibrate: 'th-th-this'
**Example**: This, that, these, those - all start with tongue between teeth

#### 4. "three" /θri/
**Common errors**: tree, free
**Tip**: Start with tongue between teeth for 'th', then add 'r'.
**Example**: Three, throw, through - 'th' then 'r'

### R Sounds

#### 5. "right" /raɪt/
**Common errors**: light, wight
**Tip**: Curl your tongue back slightly without touching the roof of your mouth. The 'r' should be strong.
**Example**: Right, read, run - practice making the 'r' sound

#### 6. "very" /ˈvɛri/
**Common errors**: wery, bery
**Tip**: Touch your top teeth with your bottom lip for the 'v' sound, then curl tongue for 'r'.
**Example**: Very good - make sure to pronounce both 'v' and 'r' clearly

### V/W Confusion

#### 7. "water" /ˈwɔtər/
**Common errors**: vater, wafer
**Tip**: Round your lips for 'w', then say 'aw' sound. Not 'v' sound.
**Example**: Water, want, will - lips should be rounded for 'w'

#### 8. "work" /wɜrk/
**Common errors**: vork
**Tip**: Start with rounded lips for 'w', not teeth-on-lip for 'v'.
**Example**: Work, word, world - all start with rounded 'w'

### Final Consonants

#### 9. "good" /ɡʊd/
**Common errors**: goo, gut
**Tip**: Don't drop the final 'd'! Touch your tongue to the roof of your mouth.
**Example**: Good food - both words end with 'd'

#### 10. "want" /wɑnt/
**Common errors**: won, wan
**Tip**: End with a clear 't' sound. Touch your tongue to the roof.
**Example**: I want it - pronounce the 't' clearly

### Vowel Sounds

#### 11. "can" /kæn/
**Common errors**: ken, con
**Tip**: Open your mouth wider for the 'a' in 'can' - it's like 'cat', not 'ken'.
**Example**: Can you? - short 'a' sound, mouth wide

#### 12. "world" /wɜrld/
**Common errors**: word, vorld
**Tip**: Say 'wer-uhld' with clear 'l' at the end. Round lips for 'w'.
**Example**: World, girl, pearl - similar 'er' sound

### Consonant Clusters

#### 13. "street" /strit/
**Common errors**: steet, seet
**Tip**: Three consonants: s-t-r together. Don't skip the 'r'.
**Example**: Street, strong, straight - keep all three sounds

## Phoneme-Level Tips

### 1. TH (voiced and unvoiced)
**Tip**: Place your tongue between your teeth and blow air gently
**Practice**: the, this, that, think, three, thought

### 2. R
**Tip**: Curl your tongue back without touching the roof of your mouth
**Practice**: right, run, very, car, bird

### 3. L
**Tip**: Touch your tongue to the ridge behind your top teeth
**Practice**: light, hello, world, fill

### 4. V
**Tip**: Touch your top teeth with your bottom lip and vibrate
**Practice**: very, voice, have, live

### 5. W
**Tip**: Round your lips like you're about to whistle
**Practice**: water, work, will, want

### 6. P
**Tip**: Press lips together then release with a puff of air
**Practice**: pay, happy, stop

### 7. B
**Tip**: Like 'p' but with voice - feel the vibration
**Practice**: boy, baby, cab

### 8. S
**Tip**: Tongue behind top teeth, make a hissing sound
**Practice**: see, this, class

### 9. Z
**Tip**: Like 's' but with voice - feel the vibration
**Practice**: zoo, please, was

### 10. SH
**Tip**: Round lips slightly, make a 'shh' sound
**Practice**: she, wish, nation

### 11. CH
**Tip**: Like 'tsh' together - chin, church, cheese
**Practice**: chair, watch, nature

### 12. J (as in judge)
**Tip**: Like 'dzh' together - jump, judge, just
**Practice**: job, age, bridge

### 13. NG
**Tip**: Sound comes through nose, tongue touches soft palate
**Practice**: sing, long, thinking

## Practice Strategies

### For TH Sounds
1. Place a finger in front of your mouth
2. You should feel air when saying 'th'
3. Practice minimal pairs: think/sink, day/they

### For R Sounds
1. Say "errr" and curl tongue back
2. Keep tongue away from roof of mouth
3. Practice: red, real, very

### For V/W
1. V: Feel teeth on lip, should vibrate
2. W: No teeth contact, lips rounded
3. Practice pairs: vest/west, vine/wine

### For Final Consonants
1. Exaggerate the final sound at first
2. Use a mirror to check mouth position
3. Practice: good-d, want-t, think-k

## Common Error Patterns by Native Language

### Spanish Speakers
- TH → T/D (think → tink)
- V → B (very → bery)
- Final consonants dropped

### Chinese Speakers
- R → L (right → light)
- L → R (light → right)
- TH → S/T (think → sink)

### Japanese Speakers
- R → L (very → vely)
- L → R (light → right)
- Final consonants modified

### Arabic Speakers
- P → B (pay → bay)
- V → F (very → fery)
- Short vowels difficult

### German Speakers
- TH → S/Z (think → sink)
- W → V (water → vater)
- V → F (very → fery)

## Quick Troubleshooting

### "I can't make the TH sound"
1. Start by placing tongue between teeth
2. Blow air gently - you should feel it
3. Practice "th-th-th" repeatedly
4. Try: three, think, that, this

### "My R sounds like L"
1. Curl tongue back (not up)
2. Don't touch roof of mouth
3. Say "errr" and feel tongue position
4. Try: red, right, run, very

### "I confuse V and W"
1. V: Bite bottom lip with top teeth
2. W: Round lips, no teeth
3. Practice pairs side-by-side
4. Try: very/wery, vine/wine

### "I drop final consonants"
1. Exaggerate endings at first
2. Practice in slow motion
3. Use a mirror to verify
4. Try: good-d, want-t, think-k

## Practice Progression

### Week 1: TH Sounds
- Focus on tongue placement
- Practice the, this, that, think
- 5-10 minutes daily

### Week 2: R and L
- Focus on tongue position
- Practice right, light, very, hello
- Compare recordings

### Week 3: V and W
- Focus on lip position
- Practice very, water, work, want
- Record and listen

### Week 4: Final Consonants
- Focus on completeness
- Practice good, want, think, world
- Check endings carefully

### Ongoing: Mix and Practice
- Combine all sounds
- Use real sentences
- Track improvement
- Celebrate progress!
